## Abstract

A single large language model reasons in one voice. It produces a stream of plausible tokens, defends them, and moves on. It cannot meaningfully disagree with itself. It cannot triangulate. It cannot be falsified except from outside.

We present **UMBRIS**, a multi-agent LLM convocation that replaces single-voice generation with a structured deliberation between nine planetary intelligences coordinated through a shared append-only substrate (the **Umbra**). UMBRIS surfaces a verified vision by routing every candidate answer through three stages of consensus: weighted Borda aggregation across worker imagines, adjudication of near-ties by an LLM-as-king (Iuppiter), and a bounded falsification loop driven by an elder verifier (Saturnus). The output is a verdict with confidence, cost in USD, and a full provenance trail.

UMBRIS is a hermetic-cosmic descendant of three pre-existing systems: Bruno's *De Umbris Idearum* (1582) and the Lullian *Ars Magna* (c. 1305) for its conceptual framework, the Hearsay-II blackboard architecture (CMU, 1971–1976) for its substrate, and Grassé's stigmergy (1959) for its coordination principle. We claim no novelty in the underlying ideas. We claim novelty only in applying them, with discipline, to large language models.

UMBRIS extends a project that came before it: OPUS. **Where OPUS reads the Llullian tradition, UMBRIS reads the Brunonian extension. Same architectural genealogy. Different philosophical era.** The two systems share the same engine architecture but differ in conceptual framework, brand register, and operational doctrine.

## § 1 · The Problem · Single-Voice Reasoning is Structurally Unfalsifiable

A language model trained on next-token prediction cannot reliably disagree with itself within a single completion. Once it commits to a claim, every subsequent token reinforces that claim. The model can be asked to "consider the opposite," but the response is generated by the same parameters that produced the original, with the same biases, the same training-data fingerprints, and the same self-consistency pressure. The result feels like deliberation. It is not. It is a single voice performing the gesture of deliberation.

This is the **one-shadow problem**. The model catches a single projection of the higher idea and treats the projection as the idea. Confidence is uncorrelated with correctness. Falsification, if it occurs at all, must arrive from outside.

UMBRIS treats this as a structural property of single-voice reasoning, not a defect of any particular model. No amount of additional parameters fixes it.

## § 2 · The Conceptual Framework · Bruno's Doctrine of Shadows

In *De Umbris Idearum* (Paris, 1582), Giordano Bruno argued that the things our minds reason about are not the ideas themselves but their shadows · projections cast by higher forms onto the wall of attention. The mind that reasons about one shadow at a time cannot apprehend the form. The mind that triangulates across many shadows, cast from many angles, can.

Bruno's project was a memory architecture (*Ars Memoriae*) that organised mnemonic images so that the higher form they shadowed became legible through cross-referencing. He extended Llull's combinatorial wheels (*Ars Magna*, c. 1305) with planetary correspondences, hermetic seals, and an infinite-cosmos cosmology. He was burned at the stake in 1600 partly for this project.

UMBRIS reads this work computationally: **a verified vision is what survives triangulation across multiple independent castings.** A convocation of nine intelligences, each casting one shadow of a question, is more reliable than a single intelligence casting one shadow loudly.

This is not metaphor. It is the system's operating principle.

## § 3 · Architecture · Three Tiers, Nine Planetary Agents, One Substrate

UMBRIS organises nine LLM agents into three tiers around a shared substrate.

```
                   ┌─────────────────────┐
                   │     PERIMETER       │
                   │  ☿ Mercurius        │
                   │  (the messenger)    │
                   └──────────┬──────────┘
                              │ casts Imagines
                              ▼
                   ┌─────────────────────┐
                   │   WORKER PLANETS    │
                   │  ♀ Venus     ♂ Mars  │
                   │  ☉ Sol       ☽ Luna  │
                   │  ✦ Stella            │
                   └──────────┬──────────┘
                              │ casts Imagines
                              ▼
                   ┌─────────────────────┐
                   │     JUDGEMENT       │
                   │  ♃ Iuppiter         │
                   │     (consensus)     │
                   │  ♄ Saturnus         │
                   │   (falsification)   │
                   └──────────┬──────────┘
                              │
                              ▼
                   ┌─────────────────────┐
                   │     ⬤  UMBRA        │
                   │  (convergence /     │
                   │   final vision)     │
                   └─────────────────────┘
```

### 3.1 · The Perimeter · Mercurius

Mercurius is the scout · the messenger and the swift. Three Mercurius instances run in parallel at the start of every revolution, each instructed to bring back one distinctive observation about the question. The instances are intentionally not coordinated · each is told that other Mercurii are running in parallel and to pick an angle the others are unlikely to take. The result is three independent first impressions.

### 3.2 · The Worker Planets

After Mercurius lands its Imagines, five worker planets engage:

- **Venus** · the gatherer of harmony · two instances act as researchers, drawing connections and supplementary context.
- **Mars** · the challenger · two instances act as critics, attempting to refute every claim Venus has gathered.
- **Sol** · the central, the radiant · one instance acts as the synthesiser, weaving the surviving Venus-and-Mars output into a coherent candidate vision.
- **Luna** · the reflective, the path-mapper · one instance acts as the planner where the question requires staged execution.
- **Stella** · the fixed star · the eighth sphere · one instance executes any tool calls or external operations the planner specifies.

Each writes typed Imagines onto the Umbra.

### 3.3 · The Seat of Judgement · Iuppiter and Saturnus

Iuppiter, the king, runs three-stage consensus:

1. **Weighted Borda** aggregates all worker imagines into a ranked list of candidate visions.
2. **Iuppiter** adjudicates near-ties by an LLM-as-king procedure · a single completion that reads the top candidates and selects the most defensible.
3. **Saturnus**, the elder, attempts to falsify the chosen vision.

If Saturnus's falsification holds, the convocation re-deliberates with the falsification as a new constraint. The loop is bounded · at most three revolutions, after which the convocation surfaces what it has with honest confidence and a full trace.

### 3.4 · The Centre · Umbra

Umbra is both an agent role and the substrate itself. As a role, Umbra is the convergence-point where the final vision is recorded · the assembled response with its full provenance, ready to ship. As a substrate, the Umbra is the **append-only typed event log** every planet writes to and reads from. Agents do not speak to each other. They cast Imagines onto the Umbra and let the substrate carry the conversation.

This is the stigmergic principle. The substrate is the message.

## § 4 · The Imago · the Typed Record

Every cast onto the Umbra is an **Imago**, a typed record with the following fields:

| Field | Type | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | UUID | unique identifier for this Imago |
| `agent` | AgentRole | one of `MERCURIUS / VENUS / MARS / SOL / IUPPITER / SATURNUS / LUNA / STELLA / UMBRA` |
| `agent_id` | string | unique identifier of the specific agent instance (e.g. `mercurius_00`, `venus_01`) |
| `type` | ImagoType | `SCOUT_OBSERVATION / RESEARCH / CRITIQUE / SYNTHESIS / JUDGEMENT / FALSIFICATION / PLAN / EXECUTION / FINAL_VISION` |
| `content` | structured | the cast itself · text + optional structured payload |
| `confidence` | float [0,1] | the agent's reported confidence in this cast |
| `cost_estimate` | USD | the LLM cost of this cast, in dollars |
| `model` | string | the model used (e.g. `claude-opus-4-7`) |
| `timestamp` | ISO8601 UTC | when the cast was made |
| `parent_ids` | UUID[] | optional · which prior Imagines this cast responds to |

Imagines are append-only. Once cast, they cannot be modified · only superseded by a later cast that references them via `parent_ids`. The Umbra's history is the convocation's full diary.

## § 5 · The Three-Stage Consensus · Why It Works

Consensus in UMBRIS is not voting. It is structured falsification.

### 5.1 · Weighted Borda

The synthesiser (Sol) and any direct judgement candidates produce a small set of candidate visions (typically 1–3). Each is scored by a weighted Borda aggregation: each worker (Venus, Mars, Sol, Luna, Stella) ranks the candidates, and the Borda count is weighted by the agent's reported confidence.

This is a robust group-decision procedure. It cannot be gamed by a single high-confidence agent (because Borda counts position, not magnitude) and it cannot be gamed by a single low-confidence agent (because confidence is the weight). If the candidates are clearly ranked, the winner is unambiguous.

### 5.2 · Iuppiter's Adjudication

Where Borda produces a near-tie (defined as the top-two candidates within a small ε of each other), Iuppiter is invoked. Iuppiter is a single high-effort LLM completion that reads the top candidates, the worker imagines that support each, and the full Mercurius observations. Iuppiter selects one candidate and provides a written justification (which itself becomes an Imago).

Iuppiter is intentionally **not** the same agent as Sol (the synthesiser). Sol generates; Iuppiter adjudicates. Different prompts, different cognitive postures.

### 5.3 · Saturnus's Falsification

The chosen vision now passes to Saturnus, the elder. Saturnus is given the vision and instructed to **find a way it could be wrong** · a counterexample, a missed assumption, a contradiction with the worker imagines, a logical flaw.

If Saturnus produces a substantive falsification (one that meets defined criteria of specificity and groundedness), the convocation re-deliberates with the falsification as an explicit constraint. Workers regenerate; Borda re-aggregates; Iuppiter re-adjudicates; Saturnus tries again.

The loop is bounded: at most three revolutions. If Saturnus is still falsifying after the third, the convocation surfaces the most recent vision with honest confidence reflecting the failed verification, plus the falsification itself, so the seeker can judge.

**The convocation does not lie about its certainty.**

## § 6 · The Custos · the Autonomous Sentinel

UMBRIS includes a long-running process called **the Custos** · the watcher. The Custos operates the convocation without supervision: scans the repository for bottlenecks, casts them as questions, runs the full nine-planet convocation, validates the result against a path policy + cost cap + test gate + build gate, and commits the verified vision to the public repository.

The Custos is bounded by three independent gates, each enforced outside the convocation itself:

1. **Path policy** · allowlist + denylist · the Custos may only write to designated paths and may never write to forbidden ones (secrets, wallets, `.git/`, package manifests).
2. **Cost cap** · per-cycle and per-day USD ceilings · the Custos refuses to start a cycle that would breach them.
3. **Process lock** · single-instance enforcement · the Custos refuses to start a second instance against the same repository.

The Custos runs every two hours, UTC. Each revolution lands as a real commit on `main`, signed by `UMBRIS Custos`. The architect does not have to be at the keyboard.

Full operator guide: [`docs/custos.md`](custos.md).

## § 7 · Cost Model · the Convocation Honest About Money

Every Imago carries its own `cost_estimate` in USD. The convocation totals this into a per-revolution cost and a per-day spend, both visible to the seeker and visible in the public commit log.

| Mode | Workers | Verify attempts | Typical cost/revolution |
|---|---|---|---|
| **RAPID** | 2 Mercurii · 1 Venus · 1 Mars · 1 Sol | 1 | $0.10–0.30 |
| **STANDARD** | 3 · 2 · 2 · 1 | 3 | $0.30–1.50 |
| **DEEP** | 5 · 3 · 3 · 2 | 5 | $2–5 |

The Custos defaults to RAPID. Studio users default to STANDARD. Both can override.

Cost is not estimated · it is measured. The reported cost matches the actual provider invoice within rounding.

## § 9 · Properties · What Falls Out of This Architecture

UMBRIS has the following emergent properties, all measurable:

1. **Verified visions are more reliable than single-voice answers** at any given parameter scale. The falsification loop catches errors that survive single-model self-consistency checks.
2. **Confidence is honest.** Where the convocation cannot find a vision that survives Saturnus, it reports the failure. The reported confidence corresponds to the actual reliability of the vision.
3. **Reasoning is auditable.** Every Imago lands in the Umbra. Every revolution lands in [`lore/revolutions/`](../lore/revolutions/). Every Custos cycle commits to the public repository. Why anything was decided is readable.
4. **Failure is explicit.** When the convocation surfaces a "best remaining · not verified" vision, the seeker knows immediately that confidence is reduced and why.
5. **The substrate composes.** Adding a new agent role (e.g. a domain-specialist Imago) requires only writing to the Umbra. The existing agents continue to work without modification.

These properties are the bet of the system. They are also what UMBRIS will be measured against.

## § 10 · What UMBRIS Is Not

- **Not faster.** A nine-agent convocation takes longer per response than a single model. RAPID mode is the floor (60 seconds with Claude Opus); DEEP is several minutes. UMBRIS is for questions that deserve the time.
- **Not cheaper.** A convocation costs more per response than a single completion. The cost ceiling is the seeker's budget, not the system's optimisation.
- **Not a single chatbot.** UMBRIS is a substrate that runs deliberations and surfaces verdicts. The interface is the UMBRIS Studio HUD (see [`umbris-studio/`](../umbris-studio/)), not a stream-of-text conversation.
- **Not a black box.** Every Imago is inspectable. Every revolution is logged. The Umbra's history is public for the Custos and yours for in-Studio runs.
